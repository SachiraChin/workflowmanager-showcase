Generate titles and descriptions for a pet video to be published on social media platforms.

CRITICAL: These must ENGAGE viewers emotionally, not just describe the video.

=== TITLES - EMOTIONAL HOOKS ===

TITLE PURPOSE:
- First thing viewers see in feeds and search
- Must stop scrolling and create immediate connection
- Should resonate with the universal experience of loving a pet

TITLE PSYCHOLOGY:
- RECOGNITION: Speak to feelings pet owners already have
- RELATABILITY: "This is MY pet" or "I feel this too"
- EMOTIONAL HOOK: Lead with feeling, not description
- CURIOSITY: Make them want to see the moment

TITLE REQUIREMENTS (5 total):
- 20-40 characters each
- Sentence case (capitalize first word and proper nouns only)
- Must trigger emotional response in pet lovers
- Ground in the specific pet type and story being told
- Avoid generic phrases that could apply to any video

=== DESCRIPTIONS - COMMUNITY INVITATION ===

DESCRIPTION PURPOSE:
- Often read AFTER viewing - influences follow/save decisions
- Extends the emotional experience
- Invites viewers into the pet-loving community
- Helps with discoverability

DESCRIPTION STRUCTURE (3 parts):

PART 1 - WELCOMING:
Open by inviting viewers into the moment. Use welcoming language ("Join us", "Welcome to", "This is"). Connect to the emotional theme.

PART 2 - RESONANCE:
Speak to what pet owners feel - the joy, the chaos, the unconditional love. Include natural keywords (pet love, furry friend, best friend, companion, etc.).

PART 3 - AFFIRMATION:
End with validation of the pet-owner bond. Leave them feeling understood as pet parents.

DESCRIPTION REQUIREMENTS (5 total):
- Follow the 3-part structure
- 3-4 complete sentences with proper punctuation
- Written in second person ("you") or universal voice
- End text with '....' (4 dots)
- Separate "emojis" field with exactly 3 pet-appropriate emojis

PUNCTUATION RULES:
- Every sentence MUST end with a period
- Use commas where grammar requires
- ASCII only - straight apostrophes (') not curly ones
- Must read naturally when spoken aloud

EMOJI GUIDELINES:
- 3 emojis per description
- Match the pet type and emotional tone
- Cat-appropriate: heart, sparkle, cat face, paw, etc.
- Dog-appropriate: heart, sparkle, dog face, paw, bone, etc.
- Vary across descriptions
